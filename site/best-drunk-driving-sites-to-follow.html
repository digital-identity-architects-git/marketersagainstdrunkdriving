The 20 Best Drunk Driving Sites to Follow

Want to help out but not sure where to start? Follow the people already doing the work. Here are 20 reputable organizations, advocates, and data sources worth your attention.

Following these accounts does three things: it keeps you informed, it puts good information into your own feed where friends can see it, and every share helps these messages travel further. That last part is the whole point. Open the ones that resonate, hit follow, and share what's useful.

01
MADD — Mothers Against Drunk Driving ↗

The original and largest victim-services and advocacy organization fighting drunk and drugged driving.

Advocacy & Victim Support
02
NHTSA ↗

The federal agency setting U.S. road-safety policy and running national impaired-driving campaigns.

Government / Data
03
Responsibility.org ↗

Foundation for Advancing Alcohol Responsibility — research and programs to eliminate drunk driving and underage drinking.

Research & Programs
04
SADD ↗

Students Against Destructive Decisions — peer-to-peer prevention built for teens and young adults.

Youth Prevention
05
We Save Lives ↗

Founded by MADD founder Candace Lightner, focused on the "3 D's" — drunk, drugged, and distracted driving.

Advocacy
06
IIHS ↗

Insurance Institute for Highway Safety — independent crash research and hard data on what actually saves lives.

Research & Data
07
GHSA ↗

Governors Highway Safety Association — the voice of state highway safety offices and their programs.

Policy / State
08
National Safety Council ↗

Century-old nonprofit working to eliminate preventable deaths, including impaired-driving fatalities.

Safety Education
09
NTSB ↗

National Transportation Safety Board — investigates crashes and issues the recommendations that change the law.

Government / Investigations
10
CDC — Impaired Driving ↗

The CDC's public-health view of impaired driving: data, risk factors, and what works to prevent it.

Public Health
11
AAA Foundation for Traffic Safety ↗

Independent research arm dedicated to saving lives through traffic-safety research and education.

Research
12
RADD ↗

The entertainment industry's road-safety nonprofit, using celebrity reach to promote safe, sober driving.

Awareness / Media
13
Vision Zero Network ↗

The movement to eliminate all traffic fatalities and severe injuries through smarter street design and policy.

Movement / Policy
14
Advocates for Highway & Auto Safety ↗

A coalition of consumer, safety, and insurance groups pushing for stronger federal safety laws.

Advocacy / Policy
15
FARS / NHTSA Data ↗

The Fatality Analysis Reporting System — the authoritative U.S. database of fatal crash data.

Data Source
16
NOYS ↗

National Organizations for Youth Safety — a coalition amplifying youth-led safety and prevention efforts.

Youth Coalition
17
Brake (Road Safety Charity) ↗

A leading international road-safety charity supporting victims and campaigning for safer roads.

International / Victim Support
18
WHO — Road Safety ↗

The World Health Organization's global view on road-traffic injury, including alcohol-impaired driving.

Global Health
19
End DUI ↗

Education and resources aimed at preventing DUIs and helping people understand the real consequences.

Education
20
Your State Highway Safety Office ↗

Every state has one. Follow yours for local laws, checkpoints, grants, and campaigns where you actually drive.

Local / State
