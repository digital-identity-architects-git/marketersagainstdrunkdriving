Whether you were hit by a drunk driver or are facing charges yourself, the attorney you choose will shape what happens next. This free, interactive guide walks you through it step by step. Tick off each item as you go — your progress is tracked at the top.

This guide is educational and is not legal advice.

1

Get clear on what kind of case you have

"Drunk driving accident" can mean very different cases, and they need different lawyers:

  • You were injured by a drunk driver — you likely need a personal injury attorney to pursue compensation.
  • You are facing DUI/DWI charges — you need a criminal defense attorney who handles DUI.
  • Both civil and criminal issues — you may need coordinated representation.

Knowing your category first saves you from hiring the wrong specialist.

2

Build a short list of specialists

General practice lawyers exist, but drunk driving cases reward specialization. Look for attorneys who:

  • Focus specifically on DUI/DWI or auto-accident injury cases.
  • Practice in the county where your case is filed.
  • Have verifiable reviews and a real track record.

Aim for 3 candidates so you can compare.

3

Check experience and track record

Experience with your specific situation matters more than years in general. Ask about:

  • Number of similar cases handled.
  • Trial experience — a lawyer who can go to trial negotiates from strength.
  • Familiarity with the science (BAC testing, field sobriety, accident reconstruction).
4

Ask the right questions in the consultation

Most offer a free consultation. Bring this list:

  • How many cases like mine have you handled, and what were the outcomes?
  • Will you personally handle my case or an associate?
  • What are the realistic best- and worst-case scenarios?
  • How and how often will you communicate with me?
  • What is your fee structure?
5

Understand the fees before you sign

Fee structures vary by case type:

  • Personal injury is often contingency (the lawyer takes a percentage only if you win).
  • Criminal defense is usually a flat or hourly fee.

Get it in writing, and make sure you know what is and isn't included (filing fees, expert witnesses, appeals).

6

Watch for red flags

Trust your gut. Walk away if you see:

  • Guarantees of a specific outcome (no honest lawyer guarantees results).
  • Pressure to sign immediately.
  • Vague or evasive answers about fees.
  • You can never reach them or only speak to staff.
7

Make your decision

Choose the attorney who combines the right experience, clear communication, fair fees, and a rapport you trust. The "best" lawyer is the one who is right for your case and whom you can work with under pressure.

However your case turns out, the safest road is the one where this never happens again. Plan a sober ride, every time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How soon should I hire an attorney after a drunk driving accident?

As soon as possible. Many DUI cases have short deadlines (often around 7-15 days) to protect your license, and evidence fades quickly in injury cases.

Do I need different lawyers for the criminal and civil sides?

Sometimes. Criminal defense and personal injury are different specialties; some firms coordinate both, but many people hire separate specialists.

What if I cannot afford an attorney?

For criminal charges you may qualify for a public defender. Many injury attorneys work on contingency, meaning no upfront cost.
